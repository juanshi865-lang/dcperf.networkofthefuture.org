About the DCperf Best Paper Award

The DCperf Best Paper Award recognizes outstanding research contributions to data center performance optimization, cloud-edge infrastructure, and future network architectures for distributed systems at global scale.

Background

The DCperf Best Paper Award was established to celebrate and encourage high-impact research in the performance optimization of modern data center and cloud-edge infrastructures. As data centers evolve from centralized facilities to distributed, multi-tier architectures spanning core data centers, regional points of presence, and edge nodes — serving latency-sensitive applications such as AI inference, real-time analytics, content delivery, and 5G/6G workloads — the need for intelligent, adaptive performance optimization has become critical.

DCperf is held in conjunction with the IEEE International Conference on Distributed Computing Systems (ICDCS), a premier IEEE conference in the field of distributed computing since 1980. Technically sponsored by the IEEE Computer Society, ICDCS brings together leading researchers from academia and industry to advance the state of the art in distributed systems and data center technologies.

Selection Process

  1. Submission. Authors submit papers addressing data center performance, cloud-edge optimization, or future network architecture topics.
  2. Peer Review. Each paper undergoes single-blind review by at least three members of the Technical Program Committee.
  3. Shortlisting. The TPC Chairs compile a shortlist of candidate papers based on review scores, originality, and technical merit.
  4. Committee Deliberation. The Organizing Committee selects the winning paper through consensus-based evaluation.
  5. Presentation. The award is conferred during the workshop. A certificate of recognition is presented to the author.

Evaluation Criteria

Originality

Does the paper present a novel approach to data center or cloud-edge performance optimization? Does it introduce new architectures, algorithms, or methodologies?

Technical Rigor

Is the methodology sound? Are results validated through production-scale experiments, realistic simulation, or formal analysis?

Practical Impact

Does the work address real operational challenges? What is its potential to influence the design and operation of next-generation data center and edge infrastructures?

Future Relevance

Does the work anticipate emerging trends in data center architecture, edge computing, or AI-driven infrastructure management?
