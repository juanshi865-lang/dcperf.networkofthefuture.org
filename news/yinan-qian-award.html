DCperf 2024 Best Paper Award:
Yinan Qian

The Organizing Committee of the International Workshop on Data Center Performance for Future Network Architectures (DCperf 2024) is pleased to announce that Yinan Qian has been selected to receive the DCperf 2024 Best Paper Award for outstanding research in adaptive, intelligent load balancing for multi-tier CDN edge networks.

⚡ Best Paper Award — DCperf 2024
Adaptive Load Balancing for Multi-Tier CDN Edge Networks: A Reinforcement Learning Approach
Author: Yinan Qian

About the Winning Paper

The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in content delivery infrastructure at global scale: how to optimally distribute traffic across multi-tier CDN edge networks under dynamically changing load conditions while meeting strict latency requirements for modern web and mobile applications.

Qian proposes a reinforcement learning framework that continuously learns and adapts load balancing policies based on real-time edge network conditions, traffic characteristics, and application-level performance requirements. The approach is validated using production-scale CDN traffic traces and demonstrates significant improvements in request distribution efficiency, reduced tail latency, and improved cache hit rates across multi-tier edge deployments — directly contributing to the performance optimization of next-generation data center and edge infrastructures.

Selection Process

The Best Paper Award was selected by the DCperf 2024 Technical Program Committee. Papers were evaluated on originality, technical quality, experimental validation, and potential for real-world impact on data center and edge infrastructure performance. The award was presented during the DCperf 2024 workshop, held in conjunction with the 44th IEEE ICDCS in Jersey City, NJ.

About DCperf

The International Workshop on Data Center Performance for Future Network Architectures (DCperf) is a specialized forum for research on the performance optimization of data center and cloud-edge infrastructures. DCperf is held in conjunction with the IEEE International Conference on Distributed Computing Systems (ICDCS), a premier IEEE conference since 1980, technically sponsored by the IEEE Computer Society.
